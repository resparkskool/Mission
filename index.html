Who this is for

You've built something. So why does it feel like you're missing?

This isn't about your job title, your income, or your relationship status. It's about a feeling underneath all of it.

"I've achieved a lot. I just don't feel like myself anymore."

Different lives. Same quiet sentence running underneath.

The real problem
DISCONNECTION

Not failure. Not burnout. Not "needing more." This is disconnection — from yourself, your emotions, the people you love, and the version of you that used to feel alive.

Respark exists to put that fire back out — by lighting a different one.

EMBER 01 — IDENTITY
"I don't know who I am outside of what I do."
Your sense of self is tied to your role, your output, your title. You feel numb more often than you feel anything. You can't remember what actually used to light you up.
You're not lost. You've just been buried under who you had to become.
EMBER 02 — EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE
"I'm either reacting or shutting down — there's no in-between."
Small things set you off in ways that surprise even you. Overwhelm is the baseline, not the exception. Calm feels unfamiliar — almost suspicious.
Your emotions aren't the problem. Your relationship with them is.
EMBER 03 — BELIEFS & PATTERNS
"I keep sabotaging the very thing I say I want."
People-pleasing that quietly drains you dry. Perfectionism that delays you instead of protecting you. A quiet voice that says you're never quite enough.
These patterns kept you safe once. They're not protecting you anymore.
EMBER 04 — RELATIONSHIPS & COMMUNICATION
"We're together, but we feel miles apart."
Conflict you avoid instead of move through. Trust that's been quietly chipped at, conversation by conversation. Feeling unseen by the people closest to you.
Distance isn't proof your relationship is broken. It's a sign something honest hasn't been said yet.
EMBER 05 — INTEGRATION
"My business is winning. Everything else is losing."
Success in one room of your life, quiet collapse in another. Constant compartmentalizing just to keep functioning. No version of "enough" that ever actually feels like enough.
One fire, not five. That's what we build back.
